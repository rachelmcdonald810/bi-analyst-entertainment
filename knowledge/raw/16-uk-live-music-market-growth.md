# A Concert Took Place Every 137 Seconds Across the UK in 2024

- **Source:** Music Business Worldwide
- **URL:** https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/a-concert-took-place-every-137-seconds-across-the-uk-in-2024-as-live-music-spend-in-the-market-jumped-9-5-to-7-2bn-according-to-new-report/
- **Author:** Music Business Worldwide Staff
- **Date:** 2025

## Content

Consumer spending on live music events across the United Kingdom jumped 9.5% year over year in 2024, reaching 6.68 billion pounds ($7.23 billion USD). This growth rate doubled the recorded music industry's 4.8% worldwide increase reported by the IFPI, demonstrating that live music continues to outpace recorded music in revenue growth. Live music revenues were approximately 2 billion pounds higher in 2024 than in 2019, the last full year before the COVID-19 pandemic.

The sheer volume of live music activity is staggering: a concert took place every 137 seconds across the UK throughout 2024. The revenue breakdown by event type shows that concerts generated 5.0 billion pounds ($5.4 billion), up 12.2% year over year, while festivals contributed 1.7 billion pounds ($1.8 billion), growing a more modest 1.9%. This disparity suggests that single-artist concert events are driving the majority of live music growth while the festival market faces headwinds.

Geographic distribution of revenue is heavily concentrated in major cities. Greater London accounted for 28.9% of total UK live music revenue, followed by Manchester at 8.1%, Glasgow at 5.7%, Birmingham at 3.5%, and Cardiff at 2.4%. This concentration has implications for data-driven touring strategies, as promoters and booking agents can use city-level streaming data to identify underserved markets outside these major hubs.

Mainstream pop acts accounted for 32.1% of consumer spend at the top 2,000 concerts, a 4.7 percentage point increase that reflects the genre's growing dominance of the live market. Major touring acts like Taylor Swift, Charli XCX, and Dua Lipa drew 23.5 million music tourists to UK events. The live music sector supported 234,000 jobs, with 78.8% being casual or freelance positions, highlighting the gig economy nature of the live entertainment workforce.

These UK market statistics illustrate the global trend of live music revenue growing faster than recorded music revenue, reinforcing the business case for analytics platforms that can connect streaming consumption patterns with live event demand forecasting.
