# Live Nation's Booming Earnings Are Not Just a Post-COVID Rebound

- **Source:** Music Business Worldwide
- **URL:** https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/live-nations-booming-earnings-arent-just-a-post-covid-rebound-and-4-other-things-we-learned-from-the-companys-q2-earnings-call/
- **Author:** Music Business Worldwide Staff
- **Date:** 2023

## Content

Live Nation's Q2 2023 earnings call provided compelling evidence that the concert industry boom extends well beyond post-COVID pent-up demand. The company reported revenue of $5.6 billion, up 27% year over year, with adjusted operating income of $590 million, up 23%. Earnings per share reached $1.02, a 55% increase. The concerts division alone generated $4.63 billion, up 28%, while ticketing revenue hit $709.3 million, up 23%, and sponsorship and advertising revenue reached $302.9 million, up 13%.

CEO Michael Rapino directly dismissed the narrative that growth merely reflects pandemic catch-up: "We don't think this is just any COVID catch-up. We think that this is going to be the time when live music on a global basis is going to have an incredible growth run." He attributed sustained growth to shifting consumer preferences toward experiences and expanding international markets.

Ticket volume data supported this thesis. Live Nation sold 117 million tickets in the first half of 2023, up 20% year over year, and Ticketmaster sales jumped 22% to 151 million tickets. CFO Joe Berchtold reported no slowdown signals, noting that per-capita spending was growing even as the company increased its number of fans per show. He estimated that experience spending tailwinds outweigh economic headwinds by approximately 10 to 1.

Dynamic pricing was expanding internationally beyond North America, representing a growing revenue optimization strategy. The secondary ticket market generates an estimated $5 billion annually for scalpers, which Live Nation views as evidence that primary market tickets are systematically underpriced. At the same time, low-end theater and amphitheater tickets remained below $35, maintaining accessibility.

Brands were increasingly redirecting advertising budgets toward live events, seeking direct consumer engagement unavailable through digital channels. This sponsorship shift represents an additional revenue stream that benefits from the same consumer preference trends driving ticket sales. The combination of attendance growth, pricing power, sponsorship demand, and international expansion creates a structural growth story that extends well beyond any temporary post-pandemic recovery.
