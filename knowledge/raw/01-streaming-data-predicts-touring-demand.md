# Pollstar's 2024 Year-End Executive Survey: Data and AI in Touring Decisions

- **Source:** Pollstar News
- **URL:** https://news.pollstar.com/2025/01/09/pollstars-2024-year-end-executive-survey-part-2/
- **Author:** Pollstar Staff
- **Date:** January 9, 2025

## Content

Pollstar's year-end executive survey for 2024 reveals how data has evolved into a cornerstone of strategy for the live entertainment industry. Industry executives reported that AI tools now offer actionable insights for market trends, fan behavior, and campaign optimization, helping refine targeting, predict ticketing patterns, and improve ROI across projects.

Streaming data has become one of the most important inputs for touring decisions. Booking agents and promoters increasingly rely on city-level listener counts from platforms like Spotify and Apple Music to gauge demand before routing tours. If an artist has a substantial number of listeners in a given city, it becomes a logical next step to identify venues in that market. The number of followers is considered an even stronger indicator than total listeners, since followers represent people who have actively chosen to engage with an artist's music and are more likely to attend a live show.

This data-driven approach represents a fundamental shift from the traditional model where tour routing depended heavily on promoter relationships and gut instinct. Today, streaming analytics provide a quantitative baseline that complements industry experience. Artists can see not just how many listeners they have in each city but also track how those numbers change over time, giving them leading indicators of where demand is growing or declining.

The survey also highlighted how data analytics helped executives make more informed decisions about venue sizing, ticket pricing, and marketing spend allocation. With the industry's total revenues on the Top 100 Worldwide Charts having grown 71% since 2019, the stakes for getting these decisions right have never been higher. Data-driven decision-making is no longer optional but essential for competitive survival in the modern touring business.
