# Guide To Routing A Tour Using Streaming Data

- **Source:** Hypebot
- **URL:** https://www.hypebot.com/hypebot/2020/04/guide-to-routing-a-tour-using-streaming-data.html
- **Author:** Hypebot Staff
- **Date:** April 2020

## Content

Streaming data has fundamentally transformed how artists and booking agents route tours. Rather than relying on promoter relationships and guesswork, modern tour routing starts with data from platforms like Spotify for Artists, Apple Music for Artists, and third-party analytics tools like Chartmetric and Soundcharts.

Location data is one of the most important data points for booking shows. Streaming platforms provide the number of unique listeners in each city and country, allowing booking agents to make realistic estimates of how many fans they can bring to a show. If there is a substantial number of listeners in a given city, it becomes a logical step to look for venues to play in that market. The number of followers is considered even more valuable than listener counts for touring purposes, because followers are those who have actively demonstrated interest in an artist's music and are more likely to attend a live event.

The practical approach to data-driven tour routing involves several steps. First, artists examine their top cities by listener count and follower count to identify geographic clusters of demand. Second, they cross-reference this data with venue availability and capacity in those markets. Third, they consider geographic proximity to create efficient routing that minimizes travel costs while maximizing attendance potential. Fourth, they monitor how listener counts in target cities change over time to identify growing versus declining markets.

Beyond basic geographic data, streaming analytics also reveal demographic information about fans, including age and gender breakdowns, which helps inform marketing and promotional strategies for each tour stop. Artists who use this data to inform their touring, content, and ad targeting consistently make better decisions than those who tour based on gut feeling alone.

Pollstar Data Cloud complements streaming data by allowing subscribers to search, view, filter, and build custom reports based on concert box office and route book data from shows performed from 1999 to present. This historical ticketing data, combined with real-time streaming data, gives booking agents a comprehensive view of market potential. The combination of streaming analytics as a leading indicator and historical ticketing data as a validation tool has become the industry standard for data-driven tour planning.
