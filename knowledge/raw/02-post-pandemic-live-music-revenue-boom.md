# Pollstar 2024 Year End Analysis: Industry Remains Strong and Steady

- **Source:** Pollstar News
- **URL:** https://news.pollstar.com/2024/12/13/2024bizanalysis/
- **Author:** Pollstar Staff
- **Date:** December 13, 2024

## Content

Pollstar's comprehensive 2024 year-end business analysis confirms that the live music industry has maintained its post-pandemic strength, even as the explosive growth phase begins to normalize. The Top 100 Worldwide Tours generated $9.5 billion in gross revenue for 2024, representing a 3.62% increase from 2023's $9.17 billion and marking the second consecutive year exceeding $9 billion. Since 2019, the last full year before the pandemic shutdown, the industry's total revenues on the Top 100 Worldwide Charts grew 71.7%, from $5.54 billion.

Taylor Swift's Eras Tour shattered the all-time touring record with an estimated $2.2 billion gross, representing approximately 11% of the total Top 100 grosses and selling over 10 million tickets. Coldplay's Music of the Spheres World Tour achieved the highest attendance ever at 10.3 million tickets and grossed over $1.14 billion, ranking as the second highest grossing tour of all time.

However, certain metrics suggest the industry is entering a plateauing or stabilization period following what Pollstar termed the "Golden Age of Live" from 2021 to 2023. Total tickets sold reached 69.9 million but showed a slight 0.27% decline. The average gross per show dipped 2% to $2.32 million, and average tickets per show fell 5.68% to 17,075. The average ticket price rose to $135.92, a 3.91% increase from the prior year and a cumulative 41.3% increase from the 2019 average of $96.17.

The North American market showed broader softness with grosses at $6.18 billion, down 6.79%, and total tickets at 45.3 million, a 7.17% decline. Despite this moderation, the industry maintained robust fundamentals with festival successes, global market expansion, and continued premium pricing power. Pollstar characterized 2024 as a year of strength and stability rather than the explosive growth seen in the immediate post-pandemic era.
