# Spotify Says It Has Helped Artists Generate $1 Billion in Ticket Sales

- **Source:** Music Business Worldwide
- **URL:** https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/spotify-says-it-has-helped-artists-generate-1bn-in-ticket-sales-to-date-now-its-teaming-up-with-seatgeek-to-drive-even-more/
- **Author:** Music Business Worldwide Staff
- **Date:** 2024

## Content

Spotify has generated over $1 billion in ticket sales for artists to date, according to Charlie Hellman, Spotify's Head of Music. This remarkable figure demonstrates the power of the streaming-to-ticketing pipeline, where fans who discover and listen to music on streaming platforms convert directly into ticket buyers for live events.

To further strengthen this pipeline, Spotify partnered with SeatGeek to enable fans to discover live events connected to the artists they follow and purchase official primary tickets without leaving the app. The integration covers 15 major US venues including State Farm Stadium, Nissan Stadium, and AT&T Stadium, with data-driven recommendations based on individual listener preferences. Abbie Riley, Spotify's Business Development Lead, called the partnership "a significant step in our mission to build a best-in-class event discovery platform."

Spotify's concert discovery platform now integrates with over 40 ticketing partners worldwide, including Ticketmaster, AXS, Eventim, Ticketek, Bandsintown, and others. Hundreds of thousands of events are automatically listed on Spotify through these partnerships. The platform experimented with direct ticket sales through "Spotify Tickets" from 2022 to 2024 but retreated from direct ticketing to focus on partnerships with established platforms.

Research on music discovery shows that after discovering new music, 42% of listeners look for tour information and 46% search for places to buy the music. YouTube leads as the top source for finding new music at 52%, followed by streaming services at 40% and TikTok at 37%. This data highlights how streaming platforms sit at a critical juncture in the fan journey from discovery to ticket purchase.

SeatGeek co-founder Russ D'Souza described the integration as helping to "remove friction at the moment of discovery, so fans can go from listening to an artist to choosing great seats in just a few steps." This frictionless discovery-to-purchase pipeline represents a fundamental shift in how the live entertainment industry connects with potential attendees, using listening behavior as a predictor of ticket demand.
