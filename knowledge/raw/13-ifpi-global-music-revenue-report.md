# IFPI Global Music Report 2025: Global Recorded Music Revenues Grew 4.8% in 2024

- **Source:** IFPI
- **URL:** https://www.ifpi.org/ifpi-amidst-highly-competitive-market-global-recorded-music-revenues-grew-4-8-in-2024/
- **Author:** IFPI
- **Date:** March 2025

## Content

The IFPI Global Music Report 2025 reveals that total global recorded music trade revenues reached $29.6 billion in 2024, up 4.8% year over year and marking the tenth consecutive year of growth. Streaming revenues exceeded $20 billion for the first time, reaching $20.4 billion and representing 69.0% of total recorded music revenues. This dominance of streaming underscores how fundamentally the industry's revenue model has shifted from physical sales and downloads to subscription-based digital consumption.

Paid subscription streaming was the key growth driver, increasing 9.5% year over year and accounting for 51.2% of the global market. Subscription streaming revenues reached $15.15 billion in 2024, while users of paid subscription accounts grew 10.6% to 752 million globally. Ad-supported streaming formats grew by a more modest 1.2%, highlighting the industry's increasing reliance on paid subscriptions rather than advertising-based models.

Performance rights revenues, which include payments from live performances and public broadcasting, reached $2.9 billion in 2024, up 5.9% year over year. This marked the fourth successive year of revenue growth in this category and represented 9.7% of total recorded music revenues globally. Physical format revenues declined 3.1% to $4.8 billion, though vinyl revenues continued their remarkable trajectory with 4.6% growth, marking the format's 18th consecutive year of expansion.

Regional growth patterns showed significant variation. The United States and Canada grew 2.1% and accounted for 40.3% of global revenues. Europe grew 8.3% and represented 29.5% of global revenues. Latin America surged 22.5% for its 15th consecutive year of growth. The Middle East and North Africa grew 22.8% as the fastest-growing region, while Sub-Saharan Africa grew 22.6% and surpassed $100 million in revenues for the first time.

IFPI CEO Victoria Oakley stated that "the essential role music plays in so many parts of our lives is evidenced in the continued growth of the global industry." The report notably does not include live music revenue in its recorded music totals, but the combined picture of $29.6 billion in recorded music plus the estimated $30+ billion global live music market illustrates the massive scale of the combined music economy and the business case for analytics that bridge both revenue streams.
