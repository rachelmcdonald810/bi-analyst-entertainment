# Spotify Revolutionizes Live Event Discovery with Concerts Near You Playlist

- **Source:** Spotify Newsroom
- **URL:** https://newsroom.spotify.com/2025-03-20/our-new-concerts-near-you-playlist-makes-it-fun-and-easy-to-discover-touring-artists/
- **Author:** Spotify
- **Date:** March 20, 2025

## Content

Spotify has launched Concerts Near You, a personalized playlist designed to bridge the gap between streaming consumption and live event attendance. The playlist is refreshed every Wednesday with 30 songs from touring artists performing nearby, using the listener's saved location from the app's Live Events section. Users can access full event details and purchase tickets directly from expandable track rows within the playlist.

Charlie Hellman, VP and Global Head of Music Product at Spotify, described the feature's dual purpose: "This new playlist not only makes it easier for fans to find shows nearby, but it also gives artists a powerful new way of selling more tickets." The feature addresses a fundamental gap in the music industry's discovery pipeline by connecting passive listening behavior with active purchase intent for live events.

Spotify's broader live events strategy has evolved significantly. The platform now integrates with over 40 ticketing partners worldwide, including Ticketmaster, AXS, Eventim, and Ticketek, to automatically list hundreds of thousands of events. Through strategic partnerships with these ticketers, promoters, and aggregators, Spotify has built a comprehensive system for surfacing upcoming concerts and festivals directly within the app, eliminating the need for fans to navigate external websites and fragmented information sources.

In October 2025, Spotify added venue following functionality, allowing listeners to follow their favorite concert locations, discover new spots nearby, and explore upcoming lineups without leaving the app. The Live Events feed offers smarter recommendations and daily updates for concert discovery. The platform has demonstrated measurable success in converting streams to ticket sales: one case study showed that by simply having events listed on Spotify, an artist sold over 2,600 additional tickets through the platform alone, reaching her 1.1 million followers.

The streaming giant's influence on live event discovery reflects a broader industry trend where streaming platforms realize that live music represents both a revenue opportunity and a way to differentiate from competitors. As the boundary between digital music consumption and live event attendance continues to blur, Spotify's position as a discovery engine for both recorded and live music gives it unique leverage in the entertainment ecosystem.
