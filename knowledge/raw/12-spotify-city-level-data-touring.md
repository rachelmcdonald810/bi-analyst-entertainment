# Chartmetric Spotify Analytics: City-Level Data for Touring Decisions

- **Source:** Chartmetric
- **URL:** https://chartmetric.com/use-cases/spotify-analytics
- **Author:** Chartmetric
- **Date:** Accessed 2025

## Content

Chartmetric provides granular Spotify analytics that go beyond the native Spotify for Artists dashboard, offering city-specific breakdowns of an artist's listener base worldwide. This geographic data is widely considered the most actionable intelligence available for tour routing and market development in the modern music industry.

The platform delivers city-specific listener information globally, showing both absolute listener counts and the percentage distribution of an artist's total audience across cities. Critically, Chartmetric also tracks how the geographic distribution of an artist's audience changes over time, providing trend data that reveals growing and declining markets. For example, if an artist's top five cities are Austin, Nashville, Denver, Portland, and Atlanta, that data directly suggests a potential touring route. City-level data functions as free market research, and artists who use it to inform touring, content, and ad targeting make consistently better decisions than those who rely on intuition.

Beyond geographic insights, Chartmetric enables artists and their teams to compare Spotify metrics like Fan Conversion Ratio, Spotify Popularity, Spotify Rank, and Playlist Reach across competitors and similar artists. Users can group similar artists by genre and key Spotify metrics including Popularity, Followers, Monthly Listeners, and Monthly Listeners to Followers Ratio. This competitive benchmarking is particularly valuable for booking agents who need to estimate an artist's drawing power relative to comparable acts that have already toured specific markets.

The Monthly Listeners to Followers Ratio is a particularly telling metric. A high ratio of listeners relative to followers suggests an artist is being discovered through playlists and algorithms but has not yet built a committed fanbase. A lower ratio where followers are a larger percentage of listeners indicates a more engaged, loyal audience that is more likely to purchase concert tickets. This distinction is crucial for promoters evaluating whether streaming numbers will translate into actual ticket sales.

The geographic audience data directly informs where promotional efforts and live performances may gain traction, while demographic breakdowns help target marketing campaigns to specific listener profiles. This combination of geographic, demographic, and engagement data from streaming platforms has become essential infrastructure for modern touring and concert promotion decisions.
